My Top 5 Dream Travel Destinations

Welcome to my travel bucket list! Below are the five places I dream of visiting someday.

1. Maasai Mara National Reserve, Kenya

Why I want to visit Maasai Mara

The Maasai Mara represents the quintessential African safari experience—vast golden savannahs teeming with wildlife beneath endless blue skies. What makes this reserve truly exceptional is the Great Migration, when approximately 1.5 million wildebeests and hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles make their annual circular journey between Tanzania's Serengeti and Kenya's Maasai Mara. Witnessing these massive herds cross the crocodile-infested Mara River would be nature's most dramatic spectacle. Beyond wildlife, I'm fascinated by the vibrant Maasai culture with their distinctive red attire, intricate beadwork, and traditional way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries despite modernization pressures. The combination of incredible biodiversity, dramatic landscapes, and living cultural heritage makes the Maasai Mara an unparalleled destination.

Things to do in Maasai Mara

  • Witness the Great Migration (typically July to October)
  • Take game drives to spot the "Big Five" ( lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, rhinoceros )
  • Experience a sunrise hot air balloon safari followed by a champagne breakfast
  • Visit a traditional Maasai village to learn about their customs and way of life
  • Photograph the spectacular African sunrises and sunsets on the savannah
  • Stay in a luxury tented camp for an authentic safari experience

2. Petra, Jordan

Why I would like to visit Petra

Petra has fascinated me ever since I first saw images of the Treasury carved into rose-colored stone. This ancient Nabataean city, dating back to around 300 B.C., represents one of humanity's most impressive architectural achievements. The idea of walking through the narrow Siq canyon, with its towering walls gradually revealing the stunning façade of the Treasury, gives me goosebumps. What's particularly fascinating is how this vast city was half-built, half-carved into the rock, creating an engineering marvel in a time without modern tools. Beyond the famous Treasury, there are over 800 monuments spread across 264 square kilometers, including tombs, temples, and an incredible Roman-style theater carved entirely from solid rock. The blend of Nabataean, Roman, and Byzantine influences creates a cultural crossroads I'm eager to explore.

Things to do in Petra

  • Hike through the Siq to witness the Treasury reveal itself
  • Climb to the Monastery for panoramic views of the surrounding mountains
  • Explore the Royal Tombs and learn about Nabataean burial practices
  • Experience Petra by Night, when the site is illuminated by thousands of candles
  • Hike to the High Place of Sacrifice for spectacular views of the ancient city
  • Visit the Petra Museum to understand the history and archaeological significance

3. Mount Kenya, Kenya

Why I want to visit Mount Kenya

Mount Kenya, Africa's second-highest peak, offers an entirely different perspective on Kenya's diverse landscapes. What fascinates me about this ancient extinct volcano is its remarkable ecological diversity—in a single journey you can experience tropical forest, bamboo zones, alpine moorland, and glaciers near the summit. The mountain creates its own weather system and supports unique plant life, including giant lobelia and groundsel plants that look like something from another planet. The cultural significance of the mountain to the Kikuyu people, who believe their god Ngai lives at the summit, adds another fascinating dimension to this natural wonder.

Things to do in Mount Kenya

  • Hike through the diverse ecosystems of the mountain
  • Climb to Point Lenana for breathtaking views
  • Explore the unique flora and fauna of the region
  • Visit the Mount Kenya Wildlife Conservancy
  • Experience the rich Kikuyu culture and traditions

4. New Zealand

Why I want to visit New Zealand

New Zealand embodies the perfect combination of breathtaking landscapes, adventure opportunities, and warm local culture. From snow-capped mountains and ancient glaciers to pristine beaches and lush rainforests, the country packs an incredible diversity of natural wonders into a relatively compact area. As a fan of The Lord of the Rings films, I'm drawn to the movie locations that showcase the country's otherworldly beauty. The Maori cultural heritage adds another fascinating dimension to explore. What appeals to me most is how accessible the wilderness is—in New Zealand, you can hike through untouched forests in the morning and enjoy world-class dining in a cosmopolitan city by evening, all while encountering some of the friendliest people on earth.

Things to do in New Zealand

  • Explore the geothermal wonders of Rotorua
  • Hike the Tongariro Alpine Crossing
  • Experience the stunning fjords of Milford Sound
  • Visit Hobbiton, the movie set from "The Lord of the Rings"
  • Experience Maori culture and traditions

5. Kyoto, Japan

Why I want to visit Kyoto

Kyoto represents the perfect blend of ancient traditions and modern Japanese culture. As the former imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, the city has preserved its cultural heritage like no other place in Japan. I'm fascinated by the over 1,600 Buddhist temples, 400 Shinto shrines, and numerous gardens that make this city a living museum. The opportunity to experience a traditional tea ceremony, witness the cherry blossoms in spring, or the vibrant autumn foliage against temple backdrops would be a dream come true. Unlike Tokyo's ultramodern atmosphere, Kyoto offers a glimpse into Japan's soul and history that I find irresistible.

Things to do in Kyoto

  • Visit the iconic Fushimi Inari Shrine with its thousands of red torii gates
  • Explore the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
  • Experience a traditional tea ceremony
  • Visit Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion
  • Stroll through the historic Gion district
  • Explore the Zen rock garden at Ryoan-ji Temple
